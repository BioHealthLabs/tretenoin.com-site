How tretinoin actually works

Tretinoin is a vitamin A derivative that speeds up how skin cells turn over. Over time this can:

  • Reduce clogged pores that lead to blackheads, whiteheads and inflamed spots.
  • Smooth fine lines and rough texture caused by sun damage.
  • Improve uneven tone when combined with sunscreen and a gentle routine.

Improvements are typically measured in months, not days. Most people go through an adjustment period of dryness and irritation while skin adapts.

Safety basics your doctor will consider

  • Current pregnancy, breastfeeding or planning pregnancy.
  • History of eczema, rosacea or very reactive skin.
  • Other prescription medicines (especially for acne or mood).
  • How much time you spend in strong sunlight and how you use sunscreen.

Always tell your prescriber about all other creams, gels and tablets you use – including “natural” products and supplements.

Tretinoin FAQ

How long until I see results?

Many dermatologists quote around 8–12 weeks to see meaningful acne improvement, and several months for photo-ageing changes. The first weeks often look worse before they look better (“purging” + irritation).

Is tretinoin stronger than retinol?

Yes. Tretinoin is an active retinoid used at much lower percentages (for example 0.025–0.1%) but is more potent than cosmetic retinol creams. That’s why it is prescription-only in many countries.

Can I buy tretinoin online without a prescription?

In many jurisdictions it is illegal or unsafe to buy prescription-strength tretinoin without a proper consultation. Be very cautious with overseas or unregulated sites. Your skin and long-term health are worth a legitimate prescription.
