Prescription retinoid guide · 2025

Understand tretinoin for acne and anti-ageing – before you start a prescription.

Tretinoin is a prescription-only retinoid. It can transform acne and sun-damage – but only when used correctly, under medical supervision. This page explains the basics and compares common ways to get treatment.

  • See the difference between tretinoin, over-the-counter retinol and adapalene.
  • Compare in-person dermatologists and online dermatology services.
  • Learn why a slow, barrier-friendly start matters more than the highest strength.

Nothing here is personal medical advice. The goal is to help you ask better questions when you speak with your own doctor or dermatologist.

★ Most common path: Online dermatologist + simple routine

Many people now start tretinoin through licensed online dermatology services, paired with a gentle cleanser, moisturiser and daily SPF. Your doctor decides if tretinoin is safe for you.

How tretinoin actually works

  • Unclogs pores and reduces acne formation.
  • Improves fine lines by increasing cell turnover.
  • Evens tone when used with sunscreen.

Results develop over months. Early dryness and irritation are normal during adaptation.

Safety basics your doctor considers

  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding.
  • History of eczema or rosacea.
  • Other prescription medications.
  • Sun exposure patterns.

Tretinoin FAQ

How long until results?

Often 8–12 weeks for acne, several months for anti-ageing.

Is tretinoin stronger than retinol?

Yes — tretinoin is far more potent and prescription-only.

Can I buy tretinoin without a prescription?

In most places it is illegal and unsafe. Always use regulated services.
